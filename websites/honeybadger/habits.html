Habits

Honey Badgers are primarily solitary animals but it is not unusual to see them hunting in pairs during breeding season. Its gestation peroid lasts for sixth months and results in two cubs that are born blind. They are blind but they communicate through whines. It is said that they can last up to 8 years in the wild, but 24 years in captivity.

They are great diggers, swimmers, and they are very intelligent animals. In 1997 a Honey Badger in India was filmed making use of a log to reach a kingfisher fledgling stuck in roots of an underground cave. Honey Badgers are one of the few species known to be capable of using tools. They are strong, ferocious, and tough. They have been known to attack much larger predators like lions and cape buffalo.

Honey Badger Doesn't Care

Please don't ever approach a honey badger.
