Early Team History

Origins

The Buccaneers became a member of fthe AFC west in 1976. They then moved on to become a part of the NFC Central. John McKay was the team's first head coach and had to biuld a team from scratch.

Frustrations

Despite the coaching of John McKay, they Bucs were unsuccessful. The fans despised John McKay because of the infamous 26 game losing streak. The 1976 Bucs were condisered one of the worst teams of all time.

Success

In 1979 the Bucs improved. The quarterback position improved steadily, the running back had a 1,000 yard rushing season, and the defense was smothering. The Bucs finished with a 10-6 recoerd.

Team Timeline

  • 1970-1973

    Expansion of the NFL

    Expansion to 28 teams was agreed upon. Tampa was the first city to get an expansion franchise. A name-the-team contest resulted in the name "Buccaneers", which is a reference to the pirates who were on Florida's gulf cost during the seventeeth century. The Buc's first home was the Tampa stadium, which sat over 72,000 fans.

  • 1983-1996

    Worst team in the league

    The Bucs' recorded an NFL-record of 12 straight seasons with ten or more losses.

  • 1987

    Back to the playoffs

    The Bucs' started the season 5-0, their first winning season since 1992.

  • 2002 Super Bowl Champions

    John Gruden helped lead the Bucanners to a superbowl victory against the Oakland Raiders by a score of 48-21.

John McKay

The first coach ever of the Tampa Bay Buccaneers. He coached the Bucs from 1976-1984.

Tony Dungy

After John McKay came other coaches, but Tony Dungy was among the more notible. 1996 was Dungy's first year. The team struggled the first season under his lead, but was able to make it to the playoffs in the 1977 season.

John Gruden

In the 2002 season, head coach Gruden led the team to a superbowl victory against the Oakland Raiders in Super Bowl XXXVI.
